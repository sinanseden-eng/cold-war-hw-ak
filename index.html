Part 2: Reading Comprehension Check

Interactive Text

Questions

1. Why did Soviet scientists share their knowledge freely?

Because there were no barriers from patent laws or corporate secrets, allowing them to accelerate progress.

2. Who did NASA work with to build rockets? Was it just the government?

They partnered with thousands of private contractors (companies) like Boeing and IBM. It was not just the government.

3. What was a specific "hidden cost" or negative consequence for each country?

USSR: Shortages of basic goods; Soyuz 1 tragedy.
USA: National anxiety; wasting money on Arms Race.

4. What did the Soviet Union focus its entire national energy on?

Scientific advancement for the benefit of the people.

5. What happened to US households as a result of the free market system?

They were flooded with new technologies like washing machines, TVs, and cars.

6. How were high achievers rewarded in the Soviet system vs. the American system?

USSR: National honor, security, respect.
USA: Unlimited personal advancement and wealth.

Source Articles

Perspective A: The Triumph of the Collective (USSR)

The Power of Planning: During the Cold War, the Soviet Union demonstrated... the USSR focused its entire national energy on scientific advancement for the benefit of the people. This centralized approach allowed for rapid industrialization.

First to the Stars: The proof of this system's success was the Space Race... By removing the barriers of patent laws and corporate secrets, Soviet scientists shared knowledge freely, accelerating progress without the need for commercial gain.

Ambition and The State: Critics often ask: "What about the individual?" In the Soviet system, ambition was redirected. High achievers were celebrated with national honor and security, not personal wealth. A scientist who excelled had a secure future and the respect of their peers.

The Burden of Winning: However, this intense competition had a dark side... While the nation won the "race" for rockets, many citizens faced shortages of basic goods. ... the rush to launch for the 50th anniversary... resulted in tragedy... cosmonaut Vladimir Komarov died due to parachute failure.

Perspective B: The Free Market Frontier (USA)

The Spirit of Enterprise: The American success story is a testament to the power of the individual... flooding households with new technologies like washing machines, televisions, and cars, improving daily life.

Winning the Moon Race: While the USSR took an early lead... NASA didn't work alone; it partnered with thousands of private contractors who competed to build the best components. This blend of public goal-setting and private innovation proved superior.

No Ceiling on Potential: The American system promised that if you worked harder than others, you could progress further. This promise of unlimited personal advancement motivated people to push boundaries...

The Cost of Constant Pressure: Yet, this high-stakes rivalry came with a heavy price... created an atmosphere of national anxiety. This "pressure cooker" environment led to the Arms Race, where trillions of dollars were spent on nuclear weapons.

Part 3: The Reporter's Desk

Grammar Practice

Task: Rewrite the direct quotes into Reported Speech. Use the verb bank.

1. President JFK: "We choose to go to the moon..."

Announced that:
JFK announced that they chose to go to the moon...

2. Soviet Engineer: "Our planning allows us to advance faster..."

Claimed that:
The engineer claimed that their planning allowed them to advance faster.

3. Igor Gouzenko: "I have secret documents... You must believe me!"

Infinitive:
Gouzenko claimed to have secret documents.
That-Clause:
Gouzenko insisted that he had secret documents.

4. RCMP Officer: "You should stay in the apartment..."

Object + Infinitive:
The officer advised him to stay in the apartment.
That-Clause:
The officer advised that he stay/should stay in the apartment.

5. Komarov: "I absolutely refuse to fly this mission!"

Refused + Inf:
Komarov refused to fly the mission.

6. US Citizen: "Will the testing damage our environment?"

Wondered if:
The citizen wondered if the testing would damage the environment.

7. Newspaper: "Soviet Union launched Sputnik!"

Reported that:
The newspaper reported that the Soviet Union had launched Sputnik.

8. NASA Director: "We succeeded because we worked with thousands..."

Explained that:
He explained that they succeeded because they worked with thousands...

9. KGB Agent: "If you betray us, there will be consequences..."

Threatened that:
He threatened that if he betrayed them, there would be consequences.

10. Scientist: "Why don't we share our research...?"

Suggested + Gerund:
The scientist suggested sharing their research.
Suggested that:
The scientist suggested that they share their research.

11. Politician: "I did not authorize the launch."

Denied + Gerund:
The politician denied authorizing the launch.
Denied that:
The politician denied that he had authorized the launch.

12. Historian: "It's a shame we spent so much money on weapons..."

Regretted + Gerund:
The historian regretted spending so much money on weapons.
Regretted that:
The historian regretted that they had spent so much money on weapons.

13. Soviet Leader: "We have decided to beat the Americans..."

Decided + Inf:
The leader decided to beat the Americans to Mars.

14. The Public: "We praise the astronauts for their bravery."

Praised + for + ing:
The public praised the astronauts for being incredibly brave.

15. Spy Handler: "I insist that you steal the blueprints..."

Insisted on + ing:
The handler insisted on stealing the blueprints.
Insisted that:
The handler insisted that he steal the blueprints.
